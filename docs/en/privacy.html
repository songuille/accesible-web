Privacy

Last updated: March 29, 2026

This page describes in general terms how data is handled in relation to the Accesible application for macOS. You should review it with legal advice if you publish it as an official policy.

Data controller

For questions about privacy and this policy: songuille@icloud.com

What Accesible is

Accesible runs on your Mac and provides local services (e.g. HTTP/WebSocket servers) for subtitles, translation, audio description and sign language video in accessible live performance, integrated with workflows that include QLab (Figure 53).

Account and subscription

The app does not use a developer-run email and password sign-in system. Full access may require a subscription managed by Apple through the in-app purchase system (StoreKit). Apple handles billing data under its own policies; the app checks on the device whether the subscription is active, without the developer receiving your Apple password through this flow.

Data on your device

Projects, media folders and settings you choose stay on your Mac (and in locations you authorise). The app may use system mechanisms (e.g. security-scoped bookmarks) to access those folders.

Network and third-party services

Some features may require an internet connection (for example, translation services using public APIs). That traffic occurs between your machine and the relevant provider according to how the app is implemented in each version. Clients on the local network connect to servers run by Accesible on your Mac according to your session configuration.

Changes

This page may be updated when the app or applicable law changes. The date at the top indicates the last revision of the text.
